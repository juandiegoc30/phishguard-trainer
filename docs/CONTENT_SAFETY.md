# Content safety guidelines

PhishGuard Trainer scenarios must stay defensive, fictional, and safe to publish.

## Allowed

- Fictional emails, SMS messages, alerts, and sender names.
- Fictional domains that are clearly not owned by real organizations.
- Defensive explanations that help learners identify warning signs.
- Public reporting resources and general incident-response guidance.

## Not allowed

- Real brand impersonation or cloned login flows.
- Requests for real credentials, MFA codes, payment data, or personal documents.
- Operational phishing instructions, evasion advice, or delivery guidance.
- Links to live suspicious sites.
- Instructions that help run a phishing campaign.

## Scenario review

Before merging a new scenario, check:

- The organization, sender, domain, and URLs are fictional.
- The learning goal is defensive and clear.
- Every suspicious element has a plain-language explanation.
- Normal elements are not misleadingly marked as suspicious.
- The scenario works in both keyboard and pointer workflows.

When in doubt, simplify the scenario and keep the educational signal.
